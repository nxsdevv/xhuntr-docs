# Which Accounts to Track

XHuntr is only as good as the accounts you feed it. Here's how to think about building your tracked list.

---

## The 4 types of accounts worth tracking

### 1. Developers you believe in
If you know the wallet or X account of a dev who has shipped before, tracking them gives you the earliest signal — they create the community before they tell anyone.

Look for: Solana devs with a track record, people who have launched tokens that performed.

### 2. Alpha callers you trust
These are CT accounts with consistent early calls. When they join a community before tweeting about it, XHuntr catches the join signal.

Look for: Callers with a verifiable track record, accounts that are often early on 5–50x tokens, people who acknowledge when they're wrong.

Avoid: Accounts that only call after the fact, paid promoters, anyone who pumps and dumps regularly.

### 3. Community aggregators
Some accounts are central nodes in information flow — they get into communities early and are present when things launch. They're not necessarily callers but their activity predicts caller activity.

Look for: Accounts that consistently appear in X communities before the CT tweet. Check their community memberships manually.

### 4. Builders / ecosystem contributors
VCs, ecosystem funds, grant programs, accelerators. When they join a community, it often signals they know something.

---

## How to find good accounts to track

**From past alerts:** When you get a Convergence Alert or CA alert, look at who was in that community. The other accounts (outside your tracked list) who were early members are candidates.

**From X community member lists:** Join X communities in your area of interest and see who the consistent early members are.

**From Twitter search:** Search for recent calls that performed — who called it first? Add them.

**From your own network:** Who in your Telegram groups has been consistently right? Find their X handle.

---

## Accounts that aren't worth tracking

- Accounts with millions of followers who call late and broadly — too much noise
- Accounts that are clearly paid promoters — their signals are manufactured, not organic
- Bots or aggregator accounts — they don't independently join communities
- Completely inactive accounts — no community activity to detect

---

## Managing your list

**Start focused:** 5–10 high-conviction accounts is more useful than 20 mediocre ones. You want signal density — alerts that correlate with price action.

**Prune regularly:** If an account hasn't triggered meaningful alerts in 30+ days, consider replacing them with someone more active.

**Track patterns:** Notice which accounts tend to be first in communities. Those are your highest-signal accounts.

**Use `/list`** in the bot to see all your tracked accounts and their current community memberships.
